OMNIMIND CLINICAL KNOWLEDGE BASE
=================================

MAJOR DEPRESSIVE DISORDER (MDD) — DSM-5 Criteria
Requires 5+ symptoms during same 2-week period. Must include depressed mood or anhedonia.
Symptoms: depressed mood most of the day; loss of interest/pleasure; significant weight change;
insomnia or hypersomnia; psychomotor agitation or retardation; fatigue or loss of energy;
worthlessness or excessive guilt; reduced concentration; recurrent thoughts of death.
Severity: Mild (5 symptoms, minor impairment); Moderate; Severe (most symptoms, marked impairment).
PHQ-9: 0-4 minimal, 5-9 mild, 10-14 moderate, 15-19 moderately severe, 20-27 severe.
PHQ-9 Q9 (suicidality) any score >0 requires immediate safety assessment.

GENERALIZED ANXIETY DISORDER (GAD) — DSM-5 Criteria
Excessive anxiety/worry more days than not for 6+ months about multiple topics.
3+ symptoms: restlessness or keyed up; easily fatigued; difficulty concentrating;
irritability; muscle tension; sleep disturbance.
GAD-7: 0-4 minimal, 5-9 mild, 10-14 moderate, 15-21 severe.
Sensitivity 89%, Specificity 82% at cutoff of 10.

PANIC DISORDER — DSM-5
Recurrent unexpected panic attacks peaking within minutes. Symptoms: palpitations,
sweating, trembling, shortness of breath, chest pain, nausea, dizziness, derealization,
fear of losing control, fear of dying, paresthesias, chills or hot flushes.

SOCIAL ANXIETY DISORDER — DSM-5
Marked fear in social situations involving scrutiny by others. Situations avoided or
endured with intense distress. Duration 6+ months.
SPIN: 0-20 none, 21-30 mild, 31-40 moderate, 41-50 severe, 51-68 very severe.

PSS-10 Scoring: 0-13 low, 14-26 moderate, 27-40 high perceived stress.
Positive items (4,5,7,8) are reverse scored.

COGNITIVE BEHAVIORAL THERAPY — Core Model
Situation → Automatic Thought → Emotion → Behavior.
Cognitive distortions: all-or-nothing thinking, catastrophizing, mind reading,
fortune telling, emotional reasoning, should statements, personalization, mental filtering.

Thought Records: 7-column format. Situation → Automatic thought → Emotions (rate 0-100%) →
Evidence FOR → Evidence AGAINST → Balanced alternative → Re-rate emotions.

Behavioral Activation: Activity monitoring → Pleasure/mastery ratings → Activity scheduling.
Action precedes motivation — schedule first, mood follows. Evidence: Martell et al. (2010).

Exposure Therapy: Fear hierarchy 0-100 SUDS. Begin ~30 SUDS. Stay until anxiety reduces 50%.
Never escape at peak anxiety — reinforces avoidance.

4-7-8 Breathing: Inhale 4, hold 7, exhale 8. Repeat 4 cycles.
Activates parasympathetic nervous system via vagal stimulation.
Evidence: Gerritsen & Band (2018), Frontiers in Human Neuroscience.

5-4-3-2-1 Grounding: 5 see, 4 touch, 3 hear, 2 smell, 1 taste.
Interrupts anxiety spiral. Evidence: DBT (Linehan, 1993); trauma-focused CBT.

Progressive Muscle Relaxation: Systematically tense (5-7 sec) and release muscle groups.
Reduces physiological anxiety including heart rate and skin conductance.
Evidence: Jacobson (1938). Daily practice 2+ weeks for lasting effect.

Scheduled Worry Time: Specific 15-minute daily window for worrying.
Postpone worries outside window. Active engagement during window.
Evidence: Borkovec et al. (1983) — evidence-based for GAD.

MINDFULNESS-BASED COGNITIVE THERAPY (MBCT)
Evidence-based for depression relapse prevention. 8-week program.
Key skill: decentering — observing thoughts as events, not facts.
"I notice I am having the thought that I am worthless" vs "I am worthless."

CRISIS ASSESSMENT — C-SSRS
Levels: 1=wish to be dead; 2=non-specific ideation; 3=ideation without plan;
4=ideation with intent, no plan; 5=ideation with specific plan and intent.
Any active ideation with plan = immediate intervention required.
Safety planning: warning signs, coping strategies, support contacts, means restriction.

THERAPEUTIC RELATIONSHIP
Strongest predictor of outcomes across all psychotherapies.
Core conditions (Rogers, 1957): empathy, unconditional positive regard, genuineness.
Rupture-repair strengthens the therapeutic alliance over time.

PAKISTAN MENTAL HEALTH CONTEXT
80%+ treatment gap — most people with mental disorders receive no treatment.
Only 400-500 registered psychiatrists for 230 million people.
Barriers: stigma ("jinn/weakness/izzat"), cost, geography, religious misconceptions.
Somatization: emotional distress expressed through physical symptoms — headaches,
chest tightness, fatigue, body pain ("dil ki bimari").
Religious coping: namaz, dua, Quran recitation are genuine protective factors — respect them.
Family: involvement expected and often protective; extended family support is culturally central.
Gender: men rarely seek help (weakness stigma); women present through somatic complaints.
Crisis resources: Umang 0317-4288665 | Rozan 051-2890505 | Edhi Foundation 115.

GAMIFICATION IN MENTAL HEALTH
Torres et al. (2023): 35% engagement increase via badges.
Streaks, progress milestones, achievement badges improve adherence.
Caution: rewards must not trivialize serious mental health content.

EMOTION RECOGNITION
Text-based: DistilBERT/RoBERTa fine-tuned on GoEmotions (27 categories, 58k samples, Google 2020).
Voice-based: RAVDESS (24 actors, 8 emotions), IEMOCAP (improvised + scripted).
Features: MFCC, pitch (F0), speech rate, energy, spectral centroid, zero crossing rate.
Multimodal fusion improves accuracy significantly over single-modality.
